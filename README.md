# Auto-Insurance-Dashboard

---

## Project Overview
This project presents an **Auto Insurance Dashboard** designed to analyze customer, vehicle, policy, and claim-related patterns for a fictional insurance provider, **PrimeShield Insurance**, operating on the U.S. West Coast. The dashboard focuses on identifying **high-risk customer segments**, understanding **claim behavior**, and uncovering **factors impacting profitability and customer lifetime value**.

---

## Business Context
PrimeShield Insurance has recently faced **profitability challenges** driven by rising claim costs, uneven risk distribution, and pricing inefficiencies. This project supports decision-making by providing data-driven insights into customer demographics, vehicle characteristics, income tiers, and policy attributes that influence insurance performance.

---

## Objectives
- Analyze customer and vehicle attributes to understand portfolio composition  
- Identify high-risk and high-cost customer segments  
- Explore relationships between income, premiums, policy types, and claims  
- Support risk-based pricing and underwriting decisions  

---

## Dashboard Highlights
- Customer distribution by state, education, employment status, and gender  
- Vehicle insights by class, size, and ownership patterns  
- Premium and Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) analysis across demographics  
- Claim behavior trends segmented by customer, vehicle, and policy features  
- Identification of high-risk and high-value customer groups

## Key Insights Summary
- Customer concentration is highest in California  
- Agent-led onboarding is the most effective acquisition channel  
- Median-income customers show the highest CLV  
- Luxury vehicles and premium policies drive higher claim severity  
- Distinct demographic and policy patterns influence risk and profitability
